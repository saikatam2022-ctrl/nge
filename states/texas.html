What Deregulation Means
Texas deregulated its electricity market in 2002, letting businesses choose their retail electricity provider instead of being locked into a single utility's rate. Your wires, grid reliability, and emergency response all remain exactly the same — you simply choose who bills you, and at what price.
In effect since 2002
⚡
Why Texas Is Different
Texas operates ERCOT — the largest competitive electricity market in the United States, covering 90% of the state's load. With 20+ licensed retail electricity providers competing for commercial contracts, there is more supplier choice and more downward rate pressure in Texas than almost any other deregulated state. That competition is what we exploit on your behalf.
ERCOT — largest competitive grid in the US
💰
What This Means for Your Business
Most Texas businesses rolled onto a variable rate at the end of their last fixed-term contract — and stayed there. Variable rates move with the wholesale market, spiking in summer heat events exactly when your HVAC, refrigeration, and equipment draw the most power. A fixed rate insulates you entirely. Our audit finds how much you're overpaying in under 10 minutes.
