How Virginia Energy Works
Why Virginia commercial accounts have competitive options
Virginia's electricity market has undergone significant restructuring. Commercial accounts meeting load thresholds can access competitive retail electricity suppliers — and most businesses haven't taken advantage of this.
🏛️
Virginia's Competitive Market
Virginia passed the Virginia Electric Utility Restructuring Act in 1999 and has evolved its energy policy over two decades. Commercial customers above certain demand thresholds are eligible to participate in Virginia's competitive retail electricity market, choosing from licensed retail energy suppliers rather than defaulting to Dominion Energy Virginia's regulated supply rate. Your utility still owns and operates the distribution wires — only the supply component is subject to competition.

⚡
Why Virginia Is Different
Virginia operates within the PJM Interconnection — the world's largest competitive wholesale electricity market. Dominion Energy Virginia (serving most of central and eastern Virginia) and Appalachian Power (serving western Virginia) are both PJM members, which means eligible commercial accounts can access the full pool of PJM-area competitive retail suppliers. Northern Virginia's dense concentration of data centers, government contractors, and professional services firms makes the region one of the highest-load commercial markets in the PJM territory.

What This Means for Your Business
Eligible Virginia commercial accounts that remain on Dominion's default supply rate are paying a price that reflects the utility's own hedged generation portfolio — often above what a competitively bid fixed-rate contract would cost. Northern Virginia's data centers and federal contractors represent some of the largest commercial loads in the region, and the gap between the default rate and a negotiated fixed contract can represent tens of thousands of dollars annually.
