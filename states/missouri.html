How Missouri Energy Works
Missouri is regulated — but savings are still on the table
Missouri operates a regulated electricity market, meaning businesses cannot choose a competing retail supplier. However, significant savings opportunities remain through rate structure optimization, demand charge management, and utility program access.
🏛️
Missouri's Regulated Market
Missouri is a regulated electricity state — your utility (Ameren Missouri in the St. Louis area, or Evergy in the Kansas City area) is the sole supplier of electricity generation and distribution in your territory. The Missouri Public Service Commission sets and approves all utility rates. You cannot switch electricity suppliers as you can in deregulated states like Illinois or Ohio.
Regulated by MO Public Service Commission
⚡
Where Savings Still Exist
Even in a regulated market, most commercial accounts are on the wrong rate schedule for their actual usage pattern. Missouri utilities offer multiple commercial tariff options — General Service, Large General Service, Time-of-Use, and Demand programs — and most businesses default to a standard rate that doesn't match their load profile. We audit your bill, identify the optimal tariff, and calculate the saving from a rate schedule change — often 10–20% with zero disruption.

What We Do for Missouri Businesses
Our Missouri service covers three areas: (1) Rate schedule optimization — moving your account to the utility tariff that best matches your load. (2) Demand charge management — identifying operational changes or equipment scheduling that lowers your peak demand and the charges tied to it. (3) Utility program enrollment — connecting you to Ameren Missouri and Evergy rebate programs, efficiency incentives, and demand response payments that most businesses never claim. All free to you.
