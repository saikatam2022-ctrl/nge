Why It Matters
What deregulation means for your business
Twelve of the thirteen markets we serve have opened their electricity generation to competition. Here's what that means in practice.
🏛️
You Choose Your Supplier
In a deregulated state, your utility still owns the wires and handles delivery, outages, and metering. But the generation component — the part that makes up the bulk of your bill — is open to competition. That means businesses can shop and switch to a licensed retail energy supplier offering a better rate, without changing anything about how power is physically delivered to their building.
Same wires. Better price.
⚡
The Default Rate Is Always Higher
Every utility in our deregulated markets offers a default rate — sometimes called the Standard Service Offer, Price to Compare, Basic Service, or Hourly Pricing. This rate is designed to be the floor, not the ceiling. It always carries overhead, hedging premiums, and regulatory cost recovery that a competitively bid contract from a retail supplier simply doesn't include. Staying on the default is the most expensive option available to you.
The default rate works against you.
💰
A Broker Runs the Competition for You
Rather than contacting 15 suppliers individually — each with their own contract formats, rate structures, and renewal terms — you work through us. We issue a competitive RFP to all licensed suppliers in your state, evaluate the responses against your load profile, and present you with the best fixed-rate option. We're paid by the winning supplier, so the entire process costs you nothing.
Free to you. Always.
