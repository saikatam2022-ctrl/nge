How Delaware Energy Works
Why Delaware businesses have more power than they realise
Delaware deregulated its electricity market in 1999. That single change means your business can shop its energy rate — and most businesses simply haven't.
🏛️
What Deregulation Means
Delaware deregulated its electricity market in 1999 under the Electricity Generation Customer Choice and Competition Act, allowing businesses to choose their retail electricity supplier instead of paying the utility's default Standard Offer Service rate. Your distribution infrastructure, grid reliability, and emergency response all remain exactly the same — you simply choose who bills you for generation, and at what price.
In effect since 1999
⚡
Why Delaware Is Different
Delaware operates within the PJM Interconnection — the world's largest competitive wholesale electricity market. Businesses served by Delmarva Power (an Exelon/Pepco Holdings utility) covering most of the state can access a broad pool of competing retail energy suppliers. Delaware's position as a corporate and financial hub means many businesses carry significant energy loads that benefit directly from competitive fixed-rate contracts.
PJM — world's largest competitive grid
💰
What This Means for Your Business
Most Delaware businesses remain on Delmarva Power's Standard Offer Service rate — a price that adjusts with wholesale market conditions and embeds the utility's full risk premium. When wholesale prices spike during summer peak demand or winter cold snaps, that cost passes directly to your bill. A fixed-rate contract eliminates that volatility entirely.
