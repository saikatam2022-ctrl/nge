How Connecticut Energy Works

What Deregulation Means
Connecticut deregulated its electricity market in 1998 under Public Act 98-28, allowing businesses to choose their retail electricity supplier instead of paying the utility's default Standard Service rate. Your distribution wires, grid reliability, and emergency response all remain exactly the same — you simply choose who bills you for generation, and at what price.
In effect since 1998
⚡
Why Connecticut Is Different
Connecticut operates within ISO-NE (Independent System Operator New England) — a competitive regional wholesale electricity market covering all six New England states. Businesses served by Eversource Energy (most of Connecticut) and United Illuminating (New Haven area) can both access a deep pool of competing retail energy suppliers. Connecticut's consistently high default utility rates make the savings opportunity particularly significant for commercial accounts.
