How Illinois Energy Works
Why Illinois businesses have more power than they realise
Illinois deregulated its electricity market in 1997. That single change means your business can shop its energy rate — and most businesses simply haven't.
🏛️
What Deregulation Means
Illinois deregulated its electricity market in 1997 under the Electric Service Customer Choice and Rate Relief Law, allowing businesses to choose their retail electricity supplier instead of paying a single utility's default rate. Your distribution wires, grid reliability, and emergency response all remain exactly the same — you simply choose who bills you, and at what price.
In effect since 1997
⚡
Why Illinois Is Different
Illinois operates within the PJM Interconnection — the world's largest competitive wholesale electricity market. Businesses in the ComEd service territory (northern Illinois including Chicago) and the Ameren territory (central and southern Illinois) can both access a deep pool of competing retail energy suppliers. That competition drives rates well below the utility default, and we negotiate on your behalf to capture that gap.
PJM — world's largest competitive grid
💰
What This Means for Your Business
Most Illinois businesses roll onto the utility's Hourly Pricing or Residential Default Service rate when their fixed-term contract ends — and never review it again. These rates track wholesale market prices, which spike during polar vortex cold snaps exactly when your heating and equipment demand is highest. A fixed-rate contract eliminates that exposure entirely. Our free audit identifies your overpayment in under 10 minutes.
