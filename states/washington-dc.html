What Deregulation Means
Washington DC deregulated its electricity market in 2001 under the DC Electric Industry Restructuring Act of 1999, allowing businesses to choose their retail electricity supplier instead of paying Pepco's (Potomac Electric Power Company) default Standard Offer Service rate. Your distribution infrastructure, metering, grid reliability, and emergency response all remain exactly the same — you simply choose who bills you for electricity generation, and at what price.
In effect since 2001
⚡
Why Washington DC Is Different
Washington DC operates within the PJM Interconnection — the world's largest competitive wholesale electricity market. The entire District is served by Pepco, an Exelon subsidiary, which retains control of distribution and metering while the generation supply component is fully open to licensed retail suppliers. DC's dense concentration of law firms, associations, embassies, government contractors, hotels, and office buildings creates significant commercial electricity loads that benefit substantially from fixed-rate competitive contracts.
PJM — world's largest competitive grid
💰
What This Means for Your Business
Most DC businesses remain on Pepco's Standard Offer Service — a rate that adjusts with wholesale PJM market prices and incorporates the utility's full hedging margin and administrative overhead. During summer peak demand periods, PJM prices spike significantly and those spikes pass directly through to your Pepco bill. A fixed-rate contract from a licensed competitive supplier eliminates that variability entirely. Our free audit identifies your exact overpayment in under 10 minutes.
