How Pennsylvania Energy Works
Why Pennsylvania businesses have more power than they realise
Pennsylvania deregulated its electricity market in 1996 — one of the first states in the nation to do so. That single change means your business can shop its energy rate — and most businesses simply haven't.
🏛️
What Deregulation Means
Pennsylvania was among the first states to open its electricity market with the Electricity Generation Customer Choice and Competition Act of 1996. This landmark law gave every commercial customer the right to choose a competitive electric generation supplier (EGS) rather than paying the utility's Price to Compare (PTC). Your distribution infrastructure, billing, outage response, and grid reliability stay exactly the same — you simply choose who generates your power, and at what price.
Pioneer deregulator since 1996
⚡
Why Pennsylvania Is Different
Pennsylvania has one of the most mature competitive energy markets in the country, with decades of supplier competition producing a deep, liquid market for commercial contracts. The state operates entirely within the PJM Interconnection, giving businesses in PECO (Philadelphia region), PPL (central and eastern PA), West Penn Power (western PA), Duquesne Light (Pittsburgh metro), and Penn Power (western PA) territories access to a wide field of competing suppliers bidding for your load.
One of the longest-running deregulated markets
💰
What This Means for Your Business
Most Pennsylvania businesses end up on the utility's default Price to Compare — a rate that resets quarterly based on wholesale procurement results. Even in a mature market, the default always carries an overhead premium above what a competitive fixed-rate contract costs. Because Pennsylvania's market has been running since 1996, suppliers have had decades to optimise pricing, meaning the gap between the default and the best fixed rate is reliably substantial. Our 10-minute audit finds that gap for your specific load profile.
