What Deregulation Means
Ohio deregulated its electricity market in 1999 under Senate Bill 3, giving commercial customers the right to choose their competitive retail electric service (CRES) provider rather than defaulting to the utility's standard offer price. Your distribution infrastructure, grid reliability, and emergency response all remain exactly the same — you simply choose who supplies your generation, and at what price.
In effect since 1999
⚡
Why Ohio Is Different
Ohio sits entirely within the PJM Interconnection — the world's largest competitive wholesale electricity market — giving businesses access to a broad field of competing retail suppliers across AEP Ohio, Ohio Edison, CEI, Toledo Edison, Duke Energy Ohio, and Dayton Power & Light service territories. More supplier competition means more downward rate pressure, and we exploit that gap on your behalf every time we run a competitive bid for your load.
PJM — world's largest competitive grid
💰
What This Means for Your Business
Most Ohio businesses end up on the utility's Standard Service Offer (SSO) — a default rate that resets periodically based on the wholesale auction and carries a risk premium built in for the utility. That premium is money you pay for convenience you don't need. A competitively bid fixed-rate contract removes it entirely. Our free 10-minute audit identifies your overpayment and shows exactly what a fixed contract saves you annually.
